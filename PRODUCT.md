# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hashem Najdawi's whole professional circle: employers/recruiters scanning for internship- and job-worthy skill proof, university community (professors and fellow students at HTU), potential freelance clients, and engineering peers arriving from LinkedIn. Most arrive cold, skim fast, and decide within seconds whether the work is credible. The site must serve a recruiter's 30-second scan and a professor's deeper project read equally well.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio presented as a "digital museum" — an archive of engineering projects (robotics, embedded systems) and web applications. It exists to convert attention into opportunity: contact for work, recognition at university, credibility with peers. Success = visitors open project details, believe the craft is real, and reach out via email/LinkedIn/GitHub.

## Brand Personality

Curated, precise, quietly confident. The museum metaphor is the voice: projects are exhibits, the visitor is walking a gallery. Dark ink surfaces, gold accents, serif display headlines, engraved-plaque typography. Refined rather than flashy — the drama comes from restraint and craft, not spectacle. Direction confirmed: **keep the museum identity, refine where it feels generic.**

## Anti-references

- Anything that reads "AI made that": default cream/dark-SaaS palettes, identical card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, gradient text, glassmorphism-by-default.
- Generic developer-portfolio templates (hero + skills bars + card grid + contact form).
- The current font stack (Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans + DM Mono) is on the saturated-defaults list; it's shipped identity for now, but it is the first candidate when "refine" work happens.

## Design Principles

1. **The museum is the message** — every element should feel curated and placarded, like exhibit labels and catalogue numbers, not marketing sections.
2. **Proof over promise** — real screenshots, real specs, real links carry the persuasion; copy stays factual and confident.
3. **One visitor, many intents** — a recruiter skimming and a professor reading deeply must both be served: fast scan hierarchy on top, depth one click away.
4. **Restraint earns the gold** — the gold accent stays scarce; when everything glows, nothing does.
5. **Craft is visible in the details** — motion, spacing, and typography precision are themselves the portfolio's first exhibit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, full keyboard operability, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, touch targets ≥44px on mobile. The custom cursor and heavy motion must never be the only way the site works.
